Hindu Prayer.

Hindu prayer — whether called puja, stuti, or simply devotion — is one of the richest and most varied prayer traditions on earth. Hinduism does not offer one god but many forms of the one divine: Brahman, the ultimate reality, expressed through countless names and faces. Prayer in this tradition is an act of relationship — a daily, often joyful offering of the self to the sacred. The Gayatri Mantra, recited for thousands of years at dawn, asks simply for the light of divine wisdom to illuminate the mind.

Bhakti — devotional love — is at the heart of Hindu prayer for many. It is not transactional but relational: the soul turning toward its source the way a flower turns toward the sun. Whether you are praying to Shiva, Lakshmi, Ganesh, or to the formless divine, the impulse is the same: to love what is greater than oneself, and to be transformed by that love. This generator helps you find words that honour that ancient impulse in the language you carry today.

Example prayers

O Divine, in whatever form You choose to meet me, I offer this moment and this intention. May my heart remain open to Your presence — in beauty, in difficulty, in the ordinary hours that make up a life. Let me act with dharma. Let me love without condition. Om Shanti.
Remover of obstacles, hear me — I do not ask for an easy path, only for the wisdom to walk it well. Let my actions today be rooted in right intention. Let my words cause no harm. And when I fall short, as I will, let me rise again without bitterness. Om.
